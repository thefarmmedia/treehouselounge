Cannabis for Beginners: Your First Visit Guide

Everything a first-timer actually needs to know before walking through the door — written for people who've never set foot in a consumption lounge, not for people who already know the lingo.

It's normal to feel nervous

Most people who walk into The Treehouse Lounge for the first time are a little nervous — even people who've used cannabis for years. A consumption lounge is a new kind of social space, and it's reasonable to not know the etiquette yet. You're not expected to. Nobody here is grading you on how "experienced" you seem.

This guide covers the basics: what strains and consumption methods actually mean, how dosing works, what BYOC requires of you, and how to pace a multi-hour visit so you have a good time instead of an overwhelming one. If you'd rather walk through the visit itself step by step, our First Visit Wizard covers check-in, the waiver, and what the room looks like when you arrive.

Strains, in plain language

You'll hear cannabis described as indica, sativa, or hybrid. Traditionally these labels were supposed to predict the effect — indica relaxing, sativa energizing — but modern research and modern breeding have made that distinction much less reliable than it used to be. Two "indica" strains can feel completely different from each other.

A more useful way to think about it: pay attention to THC percentage (higher isn't automatically better, especially as a beginner) and to terpenes — the aromatic compounds that give a strain its smell and contribute to how it feels. If a budtender or a member at the lounge describes something as "heavy" or "couch-lock," that's a signal to go slow. If you're curious about terpenes specifically, that's its own guide — ask the AI Concierge and it'll point you in the right direction.

What "BYOC" actually means

The Treehouse Lounge operates on a Bring Your Own Cannabis model. That's a deliberate legal and operational choice: the lounge itself never sells, supplies, or distributes cannabis. Members and guests purchase their own products beforehand — typically from one of Missouri's licensed dispensaries — and bring it with them to consume in the lounge.

What this means practically: plan ahead. Buy what you intend to use before you arrive. The lounge provides the space, the equipment rentals, the community, and the events — but not the product itself. Occasionally a cannabis brand will host a product launch or sampling event at the lounge; those are clearly marked and run separately from a normal visit.

You don't need to bring your own gear if you don't have any. Rental smoking equipment is available on-site for $5–$20 a day, including bowls, gravity bongs, and vaporizers like the Volcano and the Puffco Peak Pro. If you're not sure what any of those are, that's completely fine — ask a staff member when you check in and they'll point you toward something simple.

Dosing basics: start low, go slow

This is the single most useful piece of advice for a beginner, and it applies no matter what you're consuming. Cannabis affects everyone differently based on tolerance, body chemistry, and what you've eaten that day. The goal of your first visit isn't to see how much you can handle — it's to have a relaxed, social, enjoyable few hours.

  • If you're smoking or vaping flower: take one small inhale, wait several minutes, and check in with how you feel before taking another.
  • If you're using a vaporizer: start with a short draw at a lower temperature setting rather than a long pull.
  • If anyone offers you an edible at an event: wait — edibles can take 1–2 hours to take effect, and it's easy to mistakenly think "nothing's happening" and take more too soon. Give it time before deciding whether you need another dose.

There's no prize for being the most "lit" person in the room. The people who have the best time at the lounge are usually the ones who paced themselves and stayed social the whole night.

Choosing a consumption method

If you're not sure what to use, here's a quick beginner-friendly breakdown:

  • Flower (smoking): the most familiar method for most people — fast-acting, easy to control dose by dose.
  • Vaporizing: generally considered gentler on the lungs than smoking, and easy to dial in with rental devices like the Volcano.
  • Dabbing/concentrates: more potent and faster-acting — better suited to people with existing tolerance, not a first-timer's starting point.
  • Edibles: slow onset, longer duration — easy to underestimate. Start with a small piece and wait it out.

The lounge regularly runs guided sessions — sometimes called PuffPuffPass sessions — at the dab bar, where someone experienced walks newer members through the process. Those are a low-pressure way to try something new with people around who actually know what they're doing.

Pacing yourself over a long visit

A visit to the lounge isn't a quick stop — people tend to settle in for a few hours, especially on event nights. That's part of the appeal: big-screen TVs, pool and foosball, board games, a rosin press, and a living-room-style seating area mean there's plenty to do between rounds.

Treat it like you would a long evening at a friend's house: drink water, eat something beforehand, take breaks, and don't feel obligated to keep pace with people who've been coming for years. Most members are genuinely happy to slow down and explain things to someone new — the culture here leans toward community, not competition.

What to bring

  • Valid government-issued photo ID — required at check-in, no exceptions. You must be 21 or older, even with a medical card.
  • Your own cannabis, purchased ahead of time from a licensed Missouri dispensary, if you want to bring your own rather than rent on-site supplies.
  • Cash, if you're planning to purchase an annual membership — those are currently sold in person.
  • A willingness to sign a standard waiver — same as you'd find at most private clubs.

What happens next

Once you've got the basics down, the rest is straightforward: show up, check in, sign the waiver, and settle in.
